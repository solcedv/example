Maurice Ralph Hilleman







Maurice Hilleman started from humble beginnings in rural eastern Montana. By the end of his life he had become known as a person that had probably saved more lives in the modern era than any other human being. He did this by developing various vaccines to help prevent diseases in people. One of the biggest diseases he help combat was influenza.

Important points from Hilleman's life


  • 1919 - Maurice Hilleman was born in the small town of Miles City, Montana
  • 1942 - Graduated first in his class from Montana State University in Bozeman, MT
  • 1944 - Completed a doctoral degree in Microbiology at the University of Chicago
  • 1948 - Head of Respiratory Diseases which help prevent diseases in the Japan during WWII
  • 1957 - Recognized and help prevent outbreak of new influenza strain
  • 1963 - Developed vaccine to combat Mumps
  • 1981 - Developed a vaccine for Hepatitis B
  • 1986 - Developed a new vaccine for Hepatitis B
  • 2005 - Maurice Hilleman Died on April 11 at the age of 85
